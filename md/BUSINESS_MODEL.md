# RecycleLacrosse — Business Model

> Utah-based. Founded by Elijah Elliott.
> Update this file as the model evolves.

---

## Mission

Ensure no lacrosse equipment goes to waste. Broken gear is recycled into new equipment
or repurposed into fitness handles. Usable gear is donated directly to children and Utah schools.

---

## Business Structure Decision

### Option A — For-Profit LLC (Recommended to start)
- Register an LLC in Utah (~$70 one-time filing fee at corporations.utah.gov)
- You own it. You can pay yourself from handle sales.
- Can still accept gear and donate — just can't give tax-deductible receipts to donors
- Simple: takes 1–2 days to set up online
- **Best if:** you want to move fast, sell workout handles, and figure out the nonprofit angle later

### Option B — 501(c)(3) Nonprofit
- Tax-exempt status; gear donors can deduct the value of donated equipment
- Eligible for grants (sports nonprofits, environmental orgs, youth programs)
- All revenue must go back into the mission — you can pay yourself a salary, but not take profits
- Takes 3–6 months and ~$275–$600 in filing fees (IRS Form 1023)
- **Best if:** your primary goal is donations + grants, not selling handles

### Hybrid Path (Common)
Start as an LLC → sell handles → build reputation → convert to nonprofit (or create a
nonprofit arm) once you have track record for grant applications.

**You do NOT need legal docs to build or launch the website.**
Get the LLC set up when you're ready to accept money (handle sales or gear donations).

## Programs

### 1. Melt & Remake
- **Input:** Broken shafts and heads
- **Process:** Collect scraps → melt when enough accumulated → remold into new gear
- **Who does it:** Elijah will learn mold-building; find a melt partner for batch runs
- **Output:** Donated to players, youth leagues, or school programs

### 2. Workout Handles
- **Input:** Broken shafts
- **Process:** Cut to grip length → finish ends → wrap → sell
- **Price:** $25 per handle
- **Donation split:** 30% of each sale ($7.50) donated to purchase new lacrosse equipment for kids
- **Output:** Sold via website; 70% supports operations, 30% funds new gear purchases

### 3. Direct Donation
- **Input:** Gear in good condition (sticks, helmets, pads, gloves, bags)
- **Process:** Clean, inspect, size, deliver
- **Output:** Donated to Utah kids and school programs
